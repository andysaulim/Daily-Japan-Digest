Indo-Pacific

Takaichi-Modi summit expands Japan-India defense and economic security ties

PM Takaichi and Indian PM Modi concluded the annual Japan-India summit in New Delhi on July 2, with 150 firms committing $12.5 billion in investment and both sides signing deals on AI, defense, and energy. Takaichi — on her first visit to India as PM — characterized the relationship as 'strategically aligned,' with maritime security and supply chain diversification framed explicitly in the context of China concerns, per Asahi and Japan Times.

So what: The investment and defense pacts, reached on Takaichi's first India visit as PM, advance the FOIP framework and deepen supply-chain alternatives to China that Tokyo has been pursuing since the rare-earth shock of 2010.

Pattern: Japan-India annual summits have alternated between Tokyo and New Delhi; this is Takaichi's inaugural bilateral with Modi as PM, marking her first major non-G7 diplomatic trip.

Economy-BOJ

Yen hits fresh 40-year low versus dollar as BOJ rate-hike caution deepens

The yen fell to a fresh 40-year low against the dollar, per WSJ and CNN, as a government policy draft signaled caution on further BOJ rate hikes and prior intervention spending of $74 billion failed to stabilize the currency. GAO Capital told CNBC that despite the weakness, the Japanese government is unlikely to intervene soon.

So what: Yen at a 40-year low with the government signaling BOJ restraint raises pressure on the Finance Ministry to decide whether to re-enter FX markets ahead of any BOJ policy meeting.

Pattern: Japan spent $74 billion in prior FX intervention to prop up the yen, per CNBC, yet the currency has continued to weaken.

China-Japan

Japan protests China's second EEZ survey-halt demand in East China Sea

China's Coast Guard ordered a second Japanese survey vessel to stop operations in Japan's EEZ in the East China Sea, per NHK and Mainichi, prompting a formal MOFA protest. Beijing's Foreign Ministry simultaneously rejected Japan's updated Free and Open Indo-Pacific framework and told Tokyo to 'educate' its nationals on Chinese law regarding the detained Japanese nationals in Dalian.

So what: Back-to-back EEZ survey confrontations combined with the Dalian detentions and FOIP rejection represent a coordinated pressure campaign that will test MOFA's response posture heading into the House of Councillors election cycle.

Pattern: China established near-daily CCG presence in the East China Sea contiguous zone from 2016 onward; ordering survey vessels to halt extends the confrontation template from patrol incursions to active interference with scientific activities.
