Top Stories
China-Japan

Chinese warship holds rare live-fire exercise inside Japan's EEZ; Russia participated jointly; China rejects protest and contests Okinotorishima's EEZ status

A Chinese destroyer conducted a live-fire drill inside Japan's exclusive economic zone, with Russia also participating in the joint exercise; Japan lodged a formal protest through the Chief Cabinet Secretary's office on July 21. China's foreign ministry rejected Japan's protest as 'groundless,' and simultaneously asserted that Okinotorishima — the remote Pacific reef around which Japan claims a 200-nautical-mile EEZ — is merely a rock not entitled to an EEZ under international law.

So what: China's dual-track response — rejecting Japan's protest on the merits while contesting the underlying EEZ claim — directly challenges the legal basis for Japan's southern Pacific maritime jurisdiction and sets a precedent for future PLA/PLAN activity in those waters.

Pattern: China has contested Okinotorishima's EEZ status for years; today's live-fire drill is described in today's articles as the first confirmed Chinese naval live-fire exercise inside Japan's claimed EEZ near that feature, marking a qualitative escalation from prior passage and patrol activity.

Japan Times · Kyodo · Reuters · NHK World · Jiji Press · Jul 21, 2026
Economy-BOJ

Cabinet approves economic blueprint with aggressive spending shift; BOJ independence affirmed in document

The Takaichi Cabinet formally approved a new economic and fiscal policy guideline on July 21 committing Japan to an investment-led growth strategy with an aggressive fiscal spending shift, including measures to boost stocks and bonds in household assets, expand regional industry clusters, and promote AI and chip standards. The document explicitly indicates BOJ independence, even as bond-market participants express concern over fiscal sustainability — the Reuters report describes bond-market 'jitters' overshadowing the blueprint's release.

So what: The blueprint's treatment of BOJ independence will be tested during the Diet's extended session; opposition lawmakers are expected to press the administration on whether fiscal expansion is compatible with BOJ policy normalization, with the Section 122 surcharge expiry on July 24 adding trade-policy pressure to the macro picture.

Japan Times · Kyodo · Jiji Press · Reuters · Nikkei Asia · Jul 21, 2026
Politics-Diet

Takaichi cabinet approval falls below 50% for first time as imperial law backlash intensifies

The Jiji poll shows the Takaichi Cabinet's approval rate has slipped below 50% for the first time since the cabinet's inauguration, with disapproval now exceeding approval; the Mainichi poll separately records the approval rate falling into the 40% range. Both polls attribute the drop primarily to backlash over the imperial house succession law amendment that codifies male-only succession — a position that is Japan's first female prime minister's.

So what: Falling approval below 50% in the Jiji poll and into the 40s in the Mainichi poll, concurrent with a contested extended Diet session, compresses Takaichi's political margin for advancing the economic blueprint and raises the risk of intraparty pressure ahead of any future election cycle.

Bloomberg · Japan Times · UPI · Straits Times · Mainichi · Jul 21, 2026
