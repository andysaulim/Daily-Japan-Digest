US to field midrange missiles in Japan for joint drills

The United States will deploy midrange missile systems to Japan for joint exercises with the Self-Defense Forces, per Nikkei Asia. The deployment advances alliance long-range fires integration under Japan's December 2022 National Security Strategy counterstrike capability framework.

So what: The missile fielding is a concrete step in operationalizing the upgraded USFJ posture and Japan's counterstrike capability — watch for Chinese or North Korean reaction statements.

Pattern: The 2022 National Security Strategy and subsequent alliance modernization discussions explicitly targeted integrated long-range fires as a capability gap to close by FY2027.

Japan chipmaking equipment suppliers report 10% drop in China sales

Japanese chipmaking equipment suppliers recorded a 10% year-on-year decline in sales to China, per Nikkei Asia. The drop reflects the combined pressure of US-aligned export controls on advanced semiconductor manufacturing equipment and accelerating Chinese domestic substitution efforts.

So what: The revenue decline tests Japan's export-control alignment with the US — sustained losses could generate domestic industry pressure on METI ahead of any renegotiation of control parameters.

Takaichi 'charm offensive' on LDP leaders draws mixed reactions amid cabinet approval slide

PM Takaichi's intensified outreach — lunches and dinners with senior LDP figures — is generating unease among some party members, per Asahi Shimbun, even as her cabinet approval fell to 55.8% (Kyodo News), the lowest since she took office in October 2025. Multiple outlets report the socializing strategy is aimed at managing factional discontent ahead of possible electoral pressure.

So what: The approval slide and intra-party friction raise the question of whether Takaichi can sustain her legislative agenda — including the proposed cut of 45 proportional representation seats — without a consolidated LDP base.
