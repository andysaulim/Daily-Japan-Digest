Nikkei 225 posts third-largest single-day point decline on record in tech sell-off

The Nikkei 225 fell more than 5% on June 26, closing at 69,360.88 — its third-biggest one-day point drop on record — driven by a broad technology sector sell-off. Brent crude simultaneously fell 6.8% to $72.60, compounding risk-off sentiment.

M5.6 earthquake strikes Yamanashi north of Mt. Fuji; Shinkansen suspended, damage reported

A M5.6 earthquake struck Yamanashi Prefecture with its epicenter north of Mt. Fuji on June 27, suspending Shinkansen service between Tokyo and Shizuoka and generating reports of damage; experts assessed eruption risk at Mt. Fuji as low. Two tropical storms simultaneously brought heavy rain to western Japan, with Typhoon Mekkhal projected to make landfall over eastern Japan Saturday night.

So what: Simultaneous seismic and typhoon events affecting the Tokyo-Nagoya-Shizuoka corridor strain emergency response capacity and may delay infrastructure-dependent logistics and supply chains.

Kyodo News; Japan Times; NHK World; AP; Washington Post
DPRK

North Korea commissions first guided-missile destroyer Choe Hyon; Kim oversees ballistic missile and tactical nuclear strike tests

Kim Jong Un oversaw tests of ballistic missiles, a modernized multiple-launch rocket system, and a system designed to strike South Korean power stations with tactical nuclear warheads, per KCNA; North Korea simultaneously commissioned its first-in-class guided-missile destroyer, the Choe Hyon, displacing approximately 5,000 tons, and announced plans for a cruiser. The combination of surface combatant commissioning and tactical nuclear-strike testing marks a step-change in DPRK conventional and nuclear force posture.

So what: The Choe Hyon commissioning and power-station targeting test directly bear on Japan's missile defense architecture, JMSDF operational planning, and the US-Japan-ROK trilateral deterrence framework.

Pattern: North Korea has progressively demonstrated tactical nuclear targeting of ROK infrastructure since 2022; the surface combatant buildup adds a naval dimension not previously present in DPRK force structure.

Reuters; NK News; navalnews.com; DW; Straits Times
Defense

Japan to consider nationalizing defense equipment production plants

Japan's government is examining the nationalization of defense equipment manufacturing facilities to secure production capacity and supply-chain resilience as the country ramps spending toward 2% of GDP by FY2027. The option signals concern that private-sector defense producers cannot sustain the required production tempo without government ownership.

So what: Nationalization of defense plants would represent a structural shift in Japan's defense-industrial model and has direct implications for US-Japan co-production and defense technology transfer arrangements.

Jiji Press
