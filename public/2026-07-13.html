China-Japan

Japan joins 14-nation statement reaffirming 2016 South China Sea ruling; China lodges stern representations

Japan's foreign minister co-signed a joint statement with the US and 12 other nations on the 10th anniversary of the 2016 Permanent Court of Arbitration ruling, declaring China's South China Sea maritime claims have no legal basis under international law. China's foreign ministry lodged stern representations with Japan and 'strongly deplored' the Japanese foreign minister's statement, with Beijing's spokesperson asking whether the signatory countries had 'voluntarily waived' their own maritime rights.

So what: Beijing's formal demarche to Tokyo raises the bilateral temperature ahead of any resumed China-Japan diplomatic contact and intersects with the ongoing Senkaku patrol pattern.

Pattern: Japan's engagement in multilateral South China Sea statements has grown since the 2016 ruling; the 10th anniversary iteration with 14 co-signatories represents the broadest coalition to date per today's reporting.

Takaichi's first economic policy blueprint centers defense industry revival

PM Takaichi's inaugural economic policy blueprint was published, placing defense industry revitalization at its core. The document represents the Takaichi Cabinet's first integrated economic strategy since taking office in October 2025.

So what: The blueprint's defense-industry emphasis signals that Takaichi intends to align industrial policy with Japan's December 2022 National Security Strategy commitments on counterstrike capability and the 2%-of-GDP defense spending target.

Economy-BOJ

MUFG surpasses Toyota to become Japan's most valuable listed company for first time

Mitsubishi UFJ Financial Group displaced Toyota Motor to become Japan's most valuable firm by market capitalization for the first time, per Bloomberg and Jiji Press. The shift reflects a financials-led market rotation as rising interest rates boost bank earnings prospects while auto-sector valuations remain pressured.

So what: A sustained MUFG lead over Toyota would mark a structural reordering of Japan's market hierarchy not seen since the era of bank dominance, with implications for index weights and foreign investor allocation.

Technology

JAXA reusable rocket completes successful first test flight and landing

Japan's space agency JAXA conducted a successful first test flight of a reusable rocket, with the vehicle landing safely. The milestone advances Japan's development of sovereign reusable launch capability, a strategic priority for both commercial and defense-adjacent space applications.

So what: A proven reusable launch system would reduce Japan's per-launch costs and reduce dependence on foreign providers for satellite and intelligence-gathering payloads.
