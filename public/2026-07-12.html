Japan and partners mark 10th anniversary of South China Sea arbitration ruling

Japan joined the Philippines and other nations on July 12 in commemorating the 10th anniversary of the Permanent Court of Arbitration's 2016 ruling that China's South China Sea claims lack a legal basis under UNCLOS. China has consistently rejected the award and has expanded its maritime presence in disputed waters since the ruling.

So what: The anniversary provides Japan and like-minded partners a multilateral framing to reaffirm rules-based maritime order at a moment of continued Chinese pressure across the East and South China Seas.

Pattern: China declared the 2016 ruling 'null and void' at the time and has not altered its position in the decade since.

Economy-BOJ

Japan presses GPIF to deepen alternative investment allocations amid domestic-asset pivot

The Japanese government is directing the Government Pension Investment Fund — the world's largest pension fund — to expand allocations to alternative assets, a move that follows the administration's broader push to reorient pension capital away from the Abe-era outpouring of Japanese funds into overseas assets. The shift has already sparked a yen and bond rally, with USD/JPY at 161.67 as of July 10.

So what: GPIF's portfolio rebalancing at scale has direct implications for JGB yields, the yen, and the BOJ's normalization path.

Pattern: The Abe-era strategy from 2013-2014 shifted GPIF heavily toward equities and foreign assets to support Abenomics; the current reversal is the largest structural pension-capital shift since that realignment.
