Yen slides past 161, Finance Minister Katayama repeats bold-action intervention warning

USD/JPY breached 161 on June 19, nearing a 40-year low, as Finance Minister Katayama and Chief Cabinet Secretary Kihara both issued explicit intervention warnings. CNBC separately reported Japan spent over $70 billion in prior intervention and raised rates without sustainably propping up the yen, raising questions about the efficacy of further unilateral action.

So what: The verbal intervention threshold has now been crossed by two senior officials simultaneously, the combination historically preceding actual market entry.

Economy-BOJ

BOJ Governor Ueda discharged from hospital; April minutes show policymaker backed multiple hikes

The Bank of Japan confirmed Governor Ueda was discharged from hospital on June 19 and will resume duties the following week, having missed the most recent policy meeting where Deputy Governor Himino presided. Released April meeting minutes showed at least one BOJ policymaker was open to multiple rate hikes, and Himino separately flagged a risk of inflation running above the 2% target.

So what: Ueda's return and the hawkish April minutes, combined with 90% of surveyed economists forecasting another hike by December, frame the next policy meeting as a live event for further normalization.
