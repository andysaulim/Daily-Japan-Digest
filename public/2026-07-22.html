Top Stories
China-Japan

China-Russia conduct live-fire drill inside Japan's claimed EEZ; Tokyo protests, Beijing rejects

A Chinese destroyer and Russian naval vessels conducted live-fire drills inside Japan's claimed exclusive economic zone near Okinotorishima on July 21, which Japan's MOD described as the first such detected instance. Japan filed a formal protest; China's foreign ministry rejected it as 'groundless' and asserted Okinotorishima is a rock that cannot generate an EEZ under international law.

So what: Beijing's dual-track response — rejecting the protest on safety grounds while simultaneously contesting Japan's EEZ legal basis — sets a precedent that could erode the EEZ framework Tokyo relies on for the remote island chain.

Pattern: China's assertion that Okinotorishima is a 'rock' and thus not entitled to an EEZ under UNCLOS mirrors a dispute posture Beijing has employed consistently since the 2000s; the addition of live-fire drills with Russia in the contested zone marks a qualitative escalation beyond prior patrol and survey-vessel activity.

Canada joins GCAP as first observer nation, expanding Japan-UK-Italy fighter coalition

Canada formally joined the Global Combat Air Programme as its first observer nation on July 22, Jiji Press and Kyodo confirmed, broadening the Japan-United Kingdom-Italy next-generation fighter development project. Observer status precedes full membership and entails participation in development discussions without binding cost-sharing obligations.

So what: Canada's entry adds a Five Eyes partner to GCAP and raises the program's political weight ahead of any future US industrial participation or export-control alignment decisions.
