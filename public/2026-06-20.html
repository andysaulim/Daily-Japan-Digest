Japan and US launch joint island defense drill in Kyushu and Okinawa

Japan and the United States commenced a joint island defense exercise in Kyushu and Okinawa on June 20, focusing on island seizure and defense scenarios across Japan's southwestern island chain. The drill proceeds as China sustains elevated military activity in the East China Sea and as Japan advances its defense buildup toward the 2% of GDP target.

So what: The exercise's timing and geographic scope — Kyushu and Okinawa — directly covers the Nansei island arc where Japan has been expanding SDF basing and where the Senkakus are located.

China-Japan

China's key mineral exports to Japan remain at low levels in May

China's exports of key minerals to Japan stayed at low levels in May, sustaining the supply pressure that prompted Japan's G7 Evian rare-earth coordination proposal. The trend follows China's export restrictions on critical minerals that took effect in late 2024 and early 2025.

So what: Continued low mineral flows keep Japan's rare-earth diversification strategy — including the G7 proposal PM Takaichi advanced at Evian — on the critical-path agenda for Tokyo's economic security planning.

Pattern: China's use of rare-earth export controls as strategic leverage has precedent in the Sep 2010 Senkaku trawler incident, when Beijing halted rare-earth exports to Japan during the captain-detention crisis.
