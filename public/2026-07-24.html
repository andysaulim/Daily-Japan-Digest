US imposes new 12.5% forced-labor tariff on Japan; Tokyo calls it 'regrettable'

The United States imposed a new 12.5% forced-labor tariff on Japan alongside other countries, with Japan's trade minister publicly labeling the measure 'regrettable' and the government announcing renewed efforts to reduce its economic impact. Japan was hit with the same rate applied to South Korea, per reporting from the Korean economic press.

So what: The new tariff adds to the existing tariff stack on Japan — separate from the 15% auto rate under the 2025 agreement and the 50% Section 232 steel/aluminum rate — and complicates ongoing US-Japan trade negotiations.
