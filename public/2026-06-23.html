Katayama confirms FX talks with Bessent as yen hits 39-year low near 161.51

Finance Minister Katayama confirmed she held talks with US Treasury Secretary Bessent on financial markets as USD/JPY reached approximately 161.51, its weakest level in roughly 39 years. Both governments reaffirmed readiness for decisive currency action; Reuters and Bloomberg reported intervention risk rising on the back of the confirmed bilateral contact.

So what: The Katayama-Bessent exchange sets the immediate backdrop for any coordinated or unilateral Japanese FX intervention decision; the 161-handle level and the 39-year-low framing will shape market positioning going into the next BOJ policy window.

Reuters Tokyo / Bloomberg / WSJ / Jiji / Mainichi
DPRK

North Korea tests new multi-purpose missile launch system; Kim asserts nuclear-state standing

North Korea tested a new multi-purpose missile launch system, per KCNA, while Kim Jong Un publicly declared the country will exercise its position as a nuclear state and warned of nuclear war risk, pledging faster defense buildup. The test and statements follow the rejection of the G7 Evian Summit denuclearization call by Kim Yo Jong earlier this month.

So what: The new launch-system test and Kim's nuclear-state declaration are directly relevant to Japan's missile defense planning and the trilateral US-Japan-ROK deterrence framework.

Pattern: North Korea has paired weapons tests with political declarations asserting nuclear status on multiple prior occasions, including surrounding the 2022 Hwasong-12 overflight of Japan that triggered J-Alert on October 4, 2022.

KCNA via Reuters / WFMZ / AsiaOne
Politics-Diet

FT: Business elites fume as PM Takaichi shuns Japan's smoky back rooms

The Financial Times reports that Japanese business leaders are frustrated with PM Takaichi's governance style, specifically her avoidance of the informal backroom consensus-building (nemawashi) that has historically mediated corporate-government relations. The piece coincides with separate reporting that Takaichi's cabinet support rate has dipped to 55.8%, its lowest since her cabinet launched.

So what: The reported corporate-PM friction, combined with the polling dip to 55.8%, informs the political risk calculus heading into any Upper House election cycle and for major economic policy initiatives.

Japan government plans return to Imperial-era military ranks for SDF, drawing criticism
The Japanese government is planning to restore Imperial military-style rank designations for the Self-Defense Forces, a move drawing criticism from opposition parties and civil society groups who see it as symbolically retracing prewar militarism. The plan coincides with China's escalating rhetoric labeling Japan's defense buildup as 'neo-militarism.'
