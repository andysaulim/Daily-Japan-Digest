Economy-BOJ

Yen breaks ¥162, hitting 40-year low as BOJ caution and Fed rate bets weigh

The yen fell through ¥162 against the dollar on July 1 — a level not seen in approximately 40 years — driven by divergence between US Federal Reserve rate expectations and the Bank of Japan's signaled caution on further hikes. Chief Cabinet Secretary Kihara maintained unchanged intervention rhetoric, stating Japan stands 'always ready to take necessary action,' while Japan has previously spent $74 billion in yen-support operations without a sustained reversal.

So what: The ¥162 breach sets the next test for whether the Finance Ministry authorizes actual intervention, with the USD/JPY trajectory directly affecting the cost calculus for Japanese defense procurement and the real value of US tariff refunds estimated at up to $3 billion for Japan Inc.

China-Japan

China arrests two Japanese nationals in Dalian; Beijing tells Tokyo to educate nationals on Chinese law

China arrested two Japanese nationals currently held in Dalian, Jiji Press reported, in the latest in a series of detentions of Japanese citizens in China. China's Foreign Ministry, via Global Times, told Japan to 'educate and remind its nationals and companies in China to abide by Chinese laws,' declining to characterize the cases as politically motivated.

So what: The Dalian arrests add to a pattern of Chinese detentions of Japanese nationals — previously including Fuji Electric workers — that Japan's government and business community have flagged as coercive economic pressure linked to the bilateral dispute over export controls.
