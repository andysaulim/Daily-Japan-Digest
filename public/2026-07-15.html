Economy-BOJ

Takaichi's economic blueprint centers defense industry revival; PM denies bond-yield link

PM Takaichi's first economic policy blueprint places defense industry revival as a core pillar and explicitly builds the fiscal case for higher defense spending. Takaichi stated publicly that the blueprint is not the cause of rising JGB yields, which held at 1.50 percent, while the government separately announced it will affirm BOJ independence in the guidelines.

So what: The blueprint's defense-spending language will shape the FY2027 MOD budget request and feeds directly into the 2%-of-GDP commitment timeline.

Pattern: Japan's Dec 2022 National Security Strategy set the 2% of GDP defense spending target for FY2027; the current blueprint extends that trajectory into an economic industrial-policy frame.

Japan Times; Reuters; Jiji Press
Defense

Japan establishes first centralized civilian intelligence agency since WWII, PMO to lead

Japan has built a new civilian foreign intelligence agency, described by the New York Times as the first such centralized body since World War II, with the Prime Minister's Office designated as lead authority. Separately, The Guardian reported Japanese officials acknowledged a growing counter-espionage need in the wake of reporting on Russian intelligence networks operating inside Japan.

So what: The agency's PMO-led structure places intelligence coordination at the apex of the Takaichi cabinet and will affect how Japan shares intelligence with the US and allied partners under the alliance's modernization framework.

New York Times; Japan Times; The Guardian
Technology

Japan enacts law recognizing cryptocurrency as financial asset

Japan's Diet enacted legislation formally recognizing cryptocurrency as a financial asset, with NHK confirming the bill's passage. The law separates crypto from the existing payment-instruments regulatory category and establishes a new supervisory framework.

So what: The regulatory reclassification directly affects how Japanese financial institutions and the FSA supervise crypto holdings, with implications for the sector's integration into Japan's broader capital markets.

Reuters / NHK; Jiji Press
