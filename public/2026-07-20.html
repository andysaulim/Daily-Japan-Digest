China-Japan

China's heavy rare earth exports to Japan remain at zero through June 2026

China's heavy rare earth tap stayed closed to Japan through June 2026, Reuters reports, extending a supply cutoff that threatens Japanese manufacturers dependent on these materials for motors, electronics, and defense components. The continued restriction follows Beijing's broader export-control escalation targeting countries it perceives as aligned with US technology restrictions.

So what: Japan's METI and defense industrial base face continued procurement pressure for rare earth-dependent components including EV motors and precision-guided systems, with no reported resumption date.

Defense Minister Koizumi calls on Japan to debate nuclear weapons, drawing China warning

Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi called on Japan to break a post-war taboo and hold an open debate on nuclear deterrence, per Asahi Shimbun and The Defense Post. China's foreign ministry responded by warning Japan it 'should not ignore the prevailing trend on nuclear issues,' citing Japanese local assembly resolutions upholding non-nuclear principles.

So what: Koizumi's statement directly implicates Japan's Three Non-Nuclear Principles and will draw intensified scrutiny during the extended Diet session beginning this week.

Politics/Diet

Takaichi expected to struggle in extended Diet session as approval hits record low

Japan Times reports PM Takaichi faces significant headwinds as the extended Diet session opens, with her cabinet's disapproval rating having surpassed approval in the latest Jiji poll and her Mainichi approval falling to the 40% range — a record low since inauguration. Key unresolved issues include the imperial succession reform (male-only succession triggering public backlash), BOJ independence, and consumption tax cuts.

So what: The Diet session's outcome on succession law and tax policy will directly shape Takaichi's political durability and the LDP's electoral positioning ahead of any next general election.
