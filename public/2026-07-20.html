China-Japan

China's heavy rare-earth export controls remain in force against Japan through June

China's heavy rare-earth export tap stayed closed for Japan through June 2026, Reuters reported, with zero shipments reaching Japan under a supply restriction that has persisted across multiple months. The cutoff affects Japan's defense production, electric vehicle manufacturing, and semiconductor supply chains.

So what: The ongoing rare-earth cutoff coincides with Koizumi's nuclear debate call and Japan's OSA defense equipment grants, adding economic-coercion context to a week of heightened Japan-China tension.

Pattern: China previously halted rare-earth exports to Japan in September 2010 following the trawler collision near the Senkaku Islands — the current restriction mirrors that coercion mechanism.
