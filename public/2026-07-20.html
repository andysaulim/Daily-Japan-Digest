Top Stories
Defense

Koizumi calls on Japan to break taboo, debate nuclear weapons

Defense Minister Koizumi publicly called on Japan to open a debate on nuclear weapons, per Asahi Shimbun. Separately, reporting indicates Japan is internally weighing revision of its three non-nuclear principles to permit introduction of US nuclear weapons — a policy that has been official doctrine since the 1960s.

So what: The call directly raises the stakes for this week's US-Japan-ROK trilateral foreign ministers meeting in Manila and the upcoming Hiroshima and Nagasaki anniversary ceremonies in August.

Pattern: Japan's non-nuclear principles — not possessing, not producing, and not permitting introduction of nuclear weapons — date to a 1967 government declaration and have not been formally revised.

Asahi Shimbun / Chosunbiz
China-Japan

China's heavy rare-earth export tap stays closed for Japan through June

China's heavy rare-earth export restrictions on Japan remained in effect through June, per Reuters, extending a supply squeeze with direct implications for Japanese defense equipment and electronics manufacturing. No easing is reported.

So what: The continued embargo puts pressure on Japan's defense buildup timeline and semiconductor supply chains, and is a live agenda item for the Manila trilateral meeting and bilateral US-Japan economic security discussions.

Pattern: China's use of rare-earth export restrictions as diplomatic leverage has a documented precedent from September 2010, when Beijing halted rare-earth exports to Japan following the Senkaku trawler collision.
