China-Japan

China's heavy rare earth tap stays closed for Japan in June

Chinese heavy rare earth exports to Japan registered zero in June, confirming the embargo extended through at least a second consecutive month. Reuters reported the data, which covers terbium, dysprosium, and related elements critical to Japan's defense and EV motor supply chains.

So what: Zero supply for two months compresses Japanese manufacturers' rare earth inventories and intensifies Tokyo's urgency on supply-chain diversification and the US-Japan strategic minerals track.

Pattern: China previously halted rare earth exports to Japan in September 2010 following the Senkaku trawler collision, a precedent documented in the Senkaku incident timeline.

Reuters Tokyo bureau
Defense

Defense Minister Koizumi calls on Japan to debate nuclear weapons

Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi publicly called for Japan to break a longstanding political taboo and debate nuclear weapons, per Mainichi and Asahi reporting. China's foreign ministry responded the same day, warning Japan 'should not ignore the prevailing trend on nuclear issues' as local assemblies pushed to uphold Japan's three non-nuclear principles.

So what: The exchange sets the political context for the Hiroshima and Nagasaki memorial ceremonies in August and will increase pressure on Takaichi in the extended Diet session.

Mainichi Shimbun; Asahi Shimbun; Global Times (China MOFA response)
Politics-Diet

Takaichi cabinet approval slips below 50% for first time — Jiji poll

A Jiji poll found the Takaichi cabinet's approval rating fell below 50% for the first time since her inauguration in October 2025, with disapproval overtaking approval. Multiple outlets cited backlash over the Imperial House Law amendment retaining male-only succession as a primary driver.

So what: Approval below 50% in the Jiji poll, combined with the Mainichi poll showing the cabinet at the 40% range, raises the political cost of the extended Diet session and Takaichi's ability to push contentious legislation.

Japan Times; Jiji Press; Mainichi Shimbun
