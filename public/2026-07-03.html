Indo-Pacific

India-Japan unveil first defense co-development pact; over 100 deals signed at 16th Annual Summit

PM Takaichi and PM Modi concluded the 16th India-Japan Annual Summit in New Delhi, producing over 100 agreements including the first-ever Japan-India defense co-development pact, plus cooperation frameworks on AI, energy, chips, and critical minerals, with more than 230 companies attending the economic forum and $13 billion in investment commitments reported. Modi described Takaichi as a 'younger sister' and the joint statement was released by both prime ministers' offices.

So what: The first Japan-India defense co-development agreement opens a new bilateral defense industrial channel that intersects with Japan's counterstrike capability buildup and its FOIP framework, both of which China's Foreign Ministry formally rejected within hours of the summit's conclusion.

Pattern: Japan and India have held annual summits since the framework was established; the 16th edition produced the first defense co-development pact, a milestone not achieved in any prior summit.

China-Japan

China's Foreign Ministry launches coordinated rejection of Japan's updated FOIP framework

China's Foreign Ministry spokesperson issued multiple statements rejecting Japan's updated Free and Open Indo-Pacific framework, characterizing it as sowing 'division and rivalry' and urging 'Asia-Pacific stability rather than turmoil.' Beijing separately warned that India-Japan critical minerals cooperation 'should not target or harm the interests of any third party.'

So what: Beijing's coordinated multi-statement response — timed to Takaichi's summit — signals that the India-Japan defense and minerals pact is now a direct object of Chinese diplomatic pressure, with implications for Japan's critical minerals supply chain diversification from China.

Pattern: China's Foreign Ministry issued similar multi-statement pushback when Japan and Australia deepened security ties in 2022; the pattern of framing bilateral cooperation as 'bloc confrontation' is consistent across cases in the reference data.

Economy-BOJ

Japanese yen hits 40-year low; finance minister vows action

The yen reached a reported 40-year low, with USD/JPY at 161.13 on July 3, as markets assessed the BOJ as falling behind the curve on rate normalization; Nikkei Asia separately reported that the BOJ's inaction relative to rising inflation expectations is sustaining yen weakness. Japan's finance minister publicly vowed to act as needed to address currency conditions.

So what: A 40-year yen low raises the threshold for BOJ intervention signaling at the next Monetary Policy Meeting and sharpens pressure on the Finance Ministry to decide between verbal and market intervention.
