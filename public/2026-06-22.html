Defense

US deploys mid-range missile system to Japan for second time

The United States has deployed a mid-range missile system to Japan for the second time, per Asahi Shimbun. The deployment follows the first such positioning and occurs as China's state media and foreign ministry are simultaneously calling Japan's defense buildup a 'retrogression' and a 'path of no return.'

So what: The second mid-range missile deployment reinforces the credibility of US extended deterrence commitments under Article 5 and directly affects the balance of forces in the Taiwan Strait and Nansei island corridors.

Pattern: Japan's Dec 2022 National Security Strategy explicitly commits to counterstrike capability and raising defense spending toward 2% of GDP by FY2027; the US mid-range deployment is part of the broader USFJ posture modernization referenced in the 2024-2025 alliance upgrade baseline.

PM Takaichi publicly signaled acceptance of the Bank of Japan's latest rate hike, per Bloomberg, marking a departure from past friction between the Kantei and BOJ over monetary tightening. BOJ Governor Ueda was separately reported discharged from hospital on the same day.

So what: Takaichi's explicit acceptance of the hike reduces the political risk of BOJ normalization and sets a new baseline for how future rate decisions will be received at the Kantei.

China-Japan

China's rare earths curbs extend pressure on supply to Japan

China's export restrictions on rare earth elements continue to press Japan's supply chains, per Reuters. The curbs follow Beijing's post-G7 Evian displeasure with PM Takaichi's rare-earth messaging and compound the pressure tracked since the June 20 baseline.

So what: Continued rare-earth supply disruption directly affects Japan's defense manufacturing, EV supply chains, and the economic security agenda that METI has been advancing through G7 critical mineral coordination.

Pattern: China halted rare-earth exports to Japan in September 2010 following the Senkaku trawler collision incident — the precedent most directly analogous to the current supply leverage mechanism.
