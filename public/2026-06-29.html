China-Japan

China adds 20 more Japanese entities to dual-use export control list, Japan protests

China added 20 more Japanese organizations to its dual-use export control blacklist on June 29, bringing the cumulative total to at least 40 entities targeted since the current escalation began, per WSJ, Bloomberg, Financial Times, and Jiji Press. Japan's Chief Cabinet Secretary issued a formal protest and the Foreign Ministry conveyed concerns to Beijing.

So what: The expansion of China's blacklist directly affects Japanese defense-related and technology firms and raises pressure on the Takaichi government ahead of the July 1-3 India summit, where economic security supply-chain diversification is on the agenda.

Pattern: China previously halted rare-earth exports to Japan following the September 2010 Senkaku trawler collision — an early precedent for using economic levers in bilateral disputes.

Chinese Coast Guard repeatedly enters Japan's EEZ; China dismisses Tokyo's maritime claims

Chinese Coast Guard ships repeatedly entered Japan's exclusive economic zone on June 29, with Jiji Press issuing multiple updates. China's Foreign Ministry spokesperson simultaneously dismissed Japan's objections over CCG activities east of Taiwan as invalid and opposed Japan-Philippines coordination as infringement of Chinese maritime rights.

So what: The coordinated CCG intrusions and China MOFA messaging on June 29 — occurring on the same day as the dual-use blacklist expansion — constitute a multi-vector pressure pattern that Japan's National Security Secretariat and MOFA will need to address jointly.

Pattern: China's Coast Guard Law, enacted in 2021, authorized use of force in claimed waters, elevating the legal framework under which current CCG patrols operate.
