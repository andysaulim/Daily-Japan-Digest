Japan producer prices jump 7.1% in June, three-year high; JGB 10-year yield hits 29-year high

Japan's producer price index rose 7.1% year-on-year in June — the fastest pace in three years — driven by higher fuel costs and yen weakness, per Jiji Press and NHK World. The 10-year Japanese government bond yield simultaneously rose to a 29-year high, per Mainichi, tightening the environment for BOJ rate policy.

So what: The combined PPI surge and JGB yield spike directly frame the BOJ's next policy meeting decision on whether to raise the 0.50% policy rate.

Economy-BOJ

BOJ holds rates steady; government's Basic Economic Policy to codify BOJ independence as yen slides to 161.76

The BOJ kept its policy rate at 0.50% and its regional economic views unchanged across all nine districts, even as the yen weakened to 161.76 per dollar and bond yields surged, per Barron's and Jiji Press. The government's forthcoming Basic Economic Policy document will include explicit language affirming BOJ independence, per Jiji Press, in a move to address market perceptions of political pressure on the central bank.

So what: The Basic Economic Policy's BOJ-independence language sets a formal government position against political interference ahead of any future rate-hike debate.
