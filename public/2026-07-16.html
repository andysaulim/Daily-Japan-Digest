Politics-Diet

Takaichi Cabinet approval drops below 50% for first time in Jiji poll

The Jiji poll — Japan's long-running monthly survey — shows the Takaichi Cabinet's approval rate has slipped below 50% for the first time since the cabinet was inaugurated on October 21, 2025. The drop is attributed to policy backlash, including tensions over the government's economic blueprint, the bond-market rout, and the ongoing food consumption tax-cut debate.

So what: The approval decline arrives as PM Takaichi seeks a Diet session extension to finalize tax-cut legislation by early August, reducing her negotiating leverage with coalition and opposition parties.

Technology

Japan to buy Nvidia Rubin chips to build AI for robots; robotics firms join physical-AI Cosmos coalition

Japan's government has agreed to purchase Nvidia Rubin chips to power a domestic AI-for-robotics program, Bloomberg reports; separately, Fujitsu and leading Japanese robotics manufacturers have joined Nvidia's physical-AI Cosmos coalition. Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang was in Japan courting AI hardware suppliers, meeting with industry leaders.

So what: The Rubin chip purchase and coalition membership embed Japan's robotics industrial base into Nvidia's AI hardware ecosystem ahead of the government's broader AI investment push under the $550B US-Japan strategic investment framework.

US-Japan-ROK defense chiefs convene in Washington, issue trilateral statement on North Korea

The defense chiefs of the United States, South Korea, and Japan met in Washington and issued a joint trilateral press statement reaffirming cooperation on North Korean denuclearization, per the official US Joint Chiefs of Staff release. The meeting follows North Korea's declared intent to expand its nuclear force both in quality and quantity, per KCNA reporting in the prior week.

So what: The trilateral chiefs statement arrives as StratNews Global reports Japan is internally assessing nuclear-deterrence option discussions in response to North Korea's declared nuclear expansion, making the Washington meeting a direct counterpoint to Pyongyang's messaging.
