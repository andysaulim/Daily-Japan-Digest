Politics-Diet

Takaichi Cabinet approval drops below 50% for first time in Jiji Poll; PM sets early-August tax-cut deadline

The Takaichi Cabinet's approval rating fell below 50% for the first time since taking office in October 2025, according to a Jiji Poll, as PM Takaichi simultaneously announced that inter-party negotiations on a food consumption tax cut should conclude by early August and the administration plans to extend the Diet session. The Jiji result contrasts with an NHK monthly poll carried in the PM appearance tracker showing 58% approval, reflecting divergence across pollsters.

So what: The approval slide, combined with a contested Diet session extension, sets the political backdrop for the food-tax-cut decision — a fiscal commitment with direct BOJ and bond-market implications given current 10-year JGB yields at 1.50%.

DPRK

US-Japan-ROK defense chiefs issue joint statement in Washington, reaffirm trilateral unity on North Korea

The defense chiefs of the United States, Japan, and South Korea met in Washington and issued a joint press statement reaffirming trilateral cooperation against North Korea, per jcs.mil. The meeting coincides with a StratNews Global report indicating Japan is internally assessing whether to enter discussions on nuclear deterrence options in response to simultaneous DPRK nuclear-expansion declarations and China pressure.

So what: The trilateral chiefs meeting and joint statement directly precede any potential Japanese government announcement on nuclear-option deliberations — a policy threshold that would affect US extended-deterrence consultations under the alliance.

Pattern: Trilateral US-Japan-ROK defense chiefs meetings have been convened periodically in Washington since the 2010s to coordinate on DPRK contingencies; the regional tracker records KCNA's July 12, 2026 declaration of an intent to expand nuclear forces 'both in quality and quantity' as the immediate antecedent.

Technology

Japan to purchase Nvidia Rubin chips for sovereign AI initiative; Japanese robotics firms join Nvidia Cosmos coalition

Japan announced plans to purchase Nvidia's Rubin-architecture chips to build a sovereign AI capability for robotics applications, with Nvidia CEO Jensen Huang in Tokyo courting Japanese AI suppliers. Kawasaki Heavy Industries, Fujitsu, and other leading Japanese robotics companies joined Nvidia's physical-AI Cosmos coalition, and Nvidia and Kawasaki Heavy agreed to build a robot-equipped AI shipyard in Japan.

So what: Japan's Rubin-chip procurement and the Kawasaki/Nvidia shipyard deal embed Nvidia hardware into Japan's defense-adjacent industrial base at a moment when the government is expanding AI policy guidelines and power companies are planning 30 new or upgraded substations to meet AI data-center demand.
