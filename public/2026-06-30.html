Yen hits 40-year low against dollar, breaking ¥162 level

The yen fell through ¥162 per dollar on June 30, a level not seen in approximately 40 years, driven by continued dollar strength and a widening US-Japan interest rate differential. Chief Cabinet Secretary Kihara stated Japan is 'always ready to take necessary action on forex,' while Finance Minister Katayama echoed readiness to act, but no intervention was announced.

So what: The Finance Ministry's repeated but unactuated intervention rhetoric puts the credibility of Tokyo's yen-defense posture under direct market scrutiny, with the 2,566 food price hikes taking effect July 1 amplifying domestic cost-of-living pressure.

China-Japan

Japan protests as China adds 20 more entities to dual-use export control blacklist

China placed 20 additional Japanese entities on its dual-use export control blacklist on June 29-30, bringing cumulative blacklistings to at least 40 organizations, per Jiji Press and multiple outlets. Japan's Chief Cabinet Secretary lodged a formal protest; China's Foreign Ministry defended the listings and dismissed Tokyo's objections to Chinese Coast Guard activities east of Taiwan as illegitimate.

So what: The expansion of the blacklist to 40 entities — alongside continued CCG EEZ incursions — forms part of a sustained Chinese economic and maritime pressure campaign directly linked to the Takaichi administration's defense posture and Japan-Philippines maritime cooperation.

Pattern: China's use of export controls as economic leverage against Japan has a precedent in the September 2010 rare-earth export halt following the Senkaku trawler collision.

Jiji Press / Bloomberg / ABC News / Taipei Times — Jun 29-30
Economy-BOJ

Japan launches CFIUS-style foreign investment screening panel

Japan launched a cross-ministerial panel to prescreen foreign investment in Japanese companies, modeled on the US CFIUS mechanism, per Nikkei Asia and Mainichi. The panel's formation operationalizes Japan's economic security legislation and reflects concerns about strategic acquisition of sensitive domestic assets.

So what: The panel's establishment sets the procedural infrastructure for blocking or conditioning inbound investment in sectors including semiconductors, defense supply chains, and critical infrastructure — a direct complement to Japan's ongoing export-control alignment with Washington.
