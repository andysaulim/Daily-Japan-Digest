Japan and India ink $13 billion in deals, first defense co-development pact at Takaichi-Modi summit

PM Takaichi and PM Modi concluded the 16th Japan-India Annual Summit in New Delhi, signing over 100 agreements covering the first bilateral defense co-development pact, AI, energy, and lunar exploration, with 230+ companies committing $13 billion at the accompanying economic forum. Modi referred to Takaichi as his 'younger sister,' per multiple reports, while Takaichi framed the summit under Japan's Free and Open Indo-Pacific framework.

So what: The first Japan-India defense co-development agreement creates a new institutional channel for defense industrial cooperation outside the US-Japan-Australia triangle, with implications for Quad burden-sharing.

Kim Jong Un oversees cruise missile launch and weapons tests from naval destroyer Kang Kon

Kim Jong Un personally observed cruise missile launches and multiple weapons tests conducted from aboard the naval destroyer Kang Kon, per KCNA-sourced reporting carried by Reuters, the Japan Times, and the Korea JoongAng Daily. The tests follow North Korea's June commissioning of the Choe Hyon, its first-in-class 5,000-ton guided-missile destroyer, and Kim's June oversight of ballistic missile and tactical nuclear-strike system tests.

So what: Sustained DPRK naval platform and missile testing through early July raises the profile of the next US-Japan-ROK trilateral security coordination session.

Pattern: Kim's direct observation of weapons tests from a named destroyer is consistent with the pattern documented in the regional tracker since at least June 27-28, when KCNA reported similar oversight of ballistic and tactical nuclear system tests.

China warns India-Japan ties 'should not target third parties' after summit deals

China's Foreign Ministry stated that India-Japan bilateral ties 'should not target third parties' in response to the Takaichi-Modi summit outcomes, per reporting from ETV Bharat and the India News Network. Beijing's reaction specifically referenced new defense and critical minerals cooperation initiatives announced at the summit.

So what: China's formal objection to the India-Japan defense co-development pact signals Beijing will characterize Quad-adjacent bilateral arrangements as destabilizing, a framing it has applied to Japan-Philippines and Japan-Australia security agreements in prior weeks.

Pattern: China's Foreign Ministry issued at least five coordinated statements on July 3 rejecting Japan's updated FOIP framework; today's warning on India-Japan ties extends that pattern to the new bilateral defense pact.
