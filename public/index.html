Economy-BOJ

Yen hits 23-month low at 161.45, Nikkei 225 breaks 71,000 for first time; government warns on intervention

The yen fell to 161.45 per dollar on June 18, its weakest since July 2024, while the Nikkei 225 simultaneously surged 7.62% to close above 71,000 for the first time. Chief Cabinet Secretary Kihara stated Japan is ready to respond 'appropriately' to yen moves 'any time,' employing the government's standard pre-intervention language. Bloomberg reports traders are targeting 40-year yen lows as the next intervention battleground, with USD/JPY divergence driven by Federal Reserve rate-hike expectations versus the BOJ's 0.50% rate.

So what: A Jiji survey shows Japanese firms find 136.8 yen per dollar 'desirable,' placing current levels roughly 25 yen beyond the comfort zone, raising the probability of MOF-directed intervention.

Japan Times; Bloomberg; Jiji Press; Reuters
China-Japan

Defense Minister Koizumi challenges China's military spending data; Beijing rejects criticism, accuses Tokyo of 'stoking confrontation'

Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi publicly questioned the credibility of China's official defense budget at the G7 Evian Summit margins and in a BBC interview, stating Japan's defense buildup is 'critical' to preventing war. China's Foreign Ministry spokesperson Lin Jian rejected the challenge at the June 18 regular press conference, and separately accused PM Takaichi of contradicting herself by calling for dialogue 'while stoking confrontation' through G7 messaging on China's rare earth restrictions. China also lodged formal protests over Japan's handling of an embassy knife intrusion incident and over what Beijing called 'recurring malicious incidents' at its Japanese diplomatic facilities.

So what: The exchange sharpens the diplomatic backdrop ahead of any potential Japan-China foreign ministerial contact and directly links Japan's defense buildup narrative to the G7 communique language on economic coercion.

BBC; Japan Times; Bloomberg; Global Times; CGTN; China Daily HK; fmprc.gov.cn

G7 Evian: Takaichi proposes critical minerals stockpiling framework; all proposals adopted in communique

PM Takaichi proposed a G7-wide framework for stockpiling critical minerals at the Evian Summit, and announced afterward that all of her proposals were incorporated into the final G7 statement.
