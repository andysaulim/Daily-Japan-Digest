Top Stories
Defense

Japan Defense Minister Koizumi calls for nuclear weapons debate, breaking decades-long taboo

Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi publicly called on Japan to open a national debate on nuclear weapons, directly challenging the three non-nuclear principles that have governed Japanese policy since the 1960s. China's foreign ministry responded the same day, warning Japan not to ignore the 'prevailing trend' on nuclear issues amid pressure from local assemblies to uphold non-nuclear principles.

So what: The call, coming from the sitting defense minister, escalates a domestic debate that has until now been confined to opposition lawmakers and academics, and directly intersects with the July 21 ASEAN foreign ministers meeting where Japan-China tensions are already elevated.

Asahi Shimbun / Global Times (Tier 4)
Politics-Diet

Takaichi cabinet disapproval overtakes approval at record low as extended Diet session opens

Multiple polls published over the weekend show that cabinet disapproval has now exceeded approval for the Takaichi government, with approval reported as having fallen to the 40% range — the lowest since the cabinet's October 2025 inauguration. The record-low rating is attributed to public backlash over PM Takaichi's position on male-only imperial succession and the defense buildup, with thousands protesting in the streets.

So what: Takaichi enters the extended Diet session this week with weakened political capital, facing unresolved votes on BOJ governance, imperial succession, and a consumption tax cut now deferred to her decision.

Japan Times / Jiji Press / CGTN / Chosunbiz
Korea-Japan

Japanese, US, and South Korean foreign ministers to meet in Philippines at ASEAN meetings

Japan's foreign minister is scheduled to meet US Secretary of State and the South Korean foreign minister on the sidelines of ASEAN Foreign Ministers Meetings in the Philippines, Jiji Press reports. The Japan Times separately notes the Manila meeting also presents a potential China-Japan ministerial encounter at a moment of elevated bilateral tensions.

So what: The trilateral foreign ministers meeting will be the first major diplomatic contact since the joint defense-chiefs statement on DPRK denuclearization and comes as Japan-China ties are strained over the South China Sea arbitral ruling statement and Koizumi's nuclear debate call.
