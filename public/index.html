Defense Minister Koizumi calls for Japan to debate nuclear weapons, China warns against it

Defense Minister Shinjiro Koizumi called publicly on Japan to break the taboo and openly debate nuclear weapons policy, per Asahi Shimbun and Mainichi. China's foreign ministry responded by warning Japan it 'should not ignore the prevailing trend on nuclear issues,' citing pressure from Japanese local assemblies reaffirming non-nuclear principles.

So what: Koizumi's statement arrives as the extended Diet session opens and the Three Strategic Documents are under 2026 review, placing nuclear deterrence policy on the active legislative agenda.

Pattern: Japan's non-nuclear three principles — not possessing, not producing, and not permitting the introduction of nuclear weapons — have been a formal government policy since 1967; no prior defense minister has publicly called for debate on revising this framework in this explicit form per today's articles.
