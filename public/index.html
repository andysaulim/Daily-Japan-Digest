China's heavy rare-earth exports to Japan stayed closed through June

China maintained its halt on heavy rare-earth exports to Japan through June, Reuters reports, extending a supply disruption with direct downstream implications for Japanese defense electronics, EV motors, and semiconductor equipment. The closure has persisted despite Japan's repeated diplomatic calls for resumption.

So what: Prolonged rare-earth denial forces Japanese industry and the MOD to accelerate stockpiling, diversification, and domestic-processing investment — each with budget and alliance-supply-chain implications.

One year on, Trump tariffs continue battering Japan automakers

One year after the 2025 US-Japan trade agreement set Japan's headline auto tariff at 15%, Japan Times reports the tariff continues to weigh on Japanese automakers' competitiveness and earnings. The 10% Section 122 surcharge (autos excluded) is set to expire July 24, making this a key inflection point for the trade framework.

So what: The Section 122 surcharge expiry on July 24 will determine whether the effective tariff burden on non-auto Japanese exports eases or is renewed, directly affecting trade negotiation leverage ahead of any further US-Japan talks.
