Takaichi Cabinet approval falls below 50% for first time — Jiji poll

The Jiji poll released July 16 shows the Takaichi Cabinet's approval rating slipping below 50% for the first time since PM Takaichi took office in October 2025. The decline comes as the administration pushes a Diet session extension and targets an early-August decision on a food consumption tax cut.

So what: Sub-50% approval constrains Takaichi's political capital heading into the Diet session extension and upcoming tax-cut negotiations, with the NHK poll (58% approval) and Jiji poll now diverging — the gap between pollsters is itself a political data point to monitor.

Jiji Press, July 16 · Multiple outlets (Japan Times, CNA, Straits Times, nippon.com)

US-Japan-ROK defense chiefs issue joint statement reaffirming trilateral cooperation on North Korea

The defense chiefs of the United States, South Korea, and Japan convened in Washington and issued a joint trilateral statement reaffirming cooperation on North Korean denuclearization and deterrence. A concurrent StratNews Global report indicates Japan is internally assessing nuclear-deterrence option discussions in response to DPRK's declared nuclear force expansion.

So what: The joint statement and Japan's internal deterrence discussions coincide with 82 Japanese local assemblies calling on the Takaichi government to uphold the three non-nuclear principles, creating a domestic political tension that will surface in the extended Diet session.

Pattern: The US-Japan-ROK trilateral chiefs format has convened periodically since formalized cooperation deepened after the 2022 Camp David summit framework; this session addresses North Korea's declared expansion of its nuclear force 'both in quality and quantity' per KCNA reporting earlier this month.
